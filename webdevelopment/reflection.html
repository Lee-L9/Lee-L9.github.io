Personal Reflection

Throughout my journey as a Computer Science student at the University of Missouri - Columbia, I have discovered a deep passion for using my computing skills to make practical applications. When I first started in the field, I had a really broad understanding of what computer scientists do, all I thought about was developing games for people to play. However, as I learn more about computer science, I understand that developing games is only a small part of the tech field. With each project I have done, I'm learning new skills and getting experience with coding. As I continue my studies, my goal is to build a career that blends my skills in computer science with my passion for solving problems. I believe that with a strong foundation in computer science and mathematics, I can take new challenges and push the boundaries of what technology can achieve.

Through my various projects and club involvement, I have gained hands-on experience in software development, database management, and leadership. Working on the Pet Adoption Application allowed me to explore Swift and mobile app development, where I learned how to display and manage pet data efficiently. While I successfully built a functional model, I realized the importance of JSON files for data storage and retrieval. I also wish I had deeper expertise in Swift to enhance user interactions and deploy the app on real devices rather than just a built-in simulator. This project reinforced my interest in app development and inspired me to improve my knowledge of backend integration for interactive applications.

Similarly, the Transaction Database Application expanded my understanding of MySQL and Python integration for real-world applications. Building a secure login system and enabling database interactions strengthened my grasp of SQL queries and database security practices. However, the limitation of displaying information in the terminal rather than a graphical user interface made me realize the importance of user experience (UX) design. In the future, I would like to improve this project by developing a more interactive UI, allowing users to easily manage their transactions through a web-based or desktop application.
